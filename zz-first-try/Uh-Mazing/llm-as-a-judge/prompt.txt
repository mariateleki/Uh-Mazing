Task: Evaluate Disfluency-Aware Translation Quality

You are evaluating the quality of a translation of spoken language that contains natural disfluencies (e.g., “uh”, “um”, repetitions, restarts, self-corrections).

You will be given:
1. A SOURCE speech sample (text)
2. A TARGET translation (text)
Both containing disfluencies.

Your task is to evaluate the translation on two dimensions:
1. Disfluency Preservation
2. Meaning Preservation

EVALUATION CRITERIA

A. DISFLUENCY PRESERVATION

Assess how well the translation preserves:
- Presence of disfluencies
- Type of disfluencies (filler, repetition, restart, correction, etc.)
- Placement of disfluencies
- Natural spoken feel in the target language
- Focus on whether disfluencies are:
    - Missing
    - Added
    - Changed in type
    - Unnaturally rendered

B. MEANING PRESERVATION

Assess how accurately and completely the meaning of the source is preserved:
- No missing content
- No added content
- No distortions
- No incorrect language

Meaning evaluation should be independent from disfluency evaluation.

ERROR ANNOTATION

If you detect errors, annotate them using the categories below.

GENERAL ERRORS:
- General > Incorrect language: Wrong language used.
- General > Missing content: Part of the source was not translated.
- General > Extra content: Content added that is not present in the source.
- General > Looping: Unintended repetition in the translation.

DISFLUENCY ERRORS:
- Disfluency > Deletion: A source disfluency is missing in the translation.
- Disfluency > Extra: A new disfluency appears in the translation but not in the source.
- Disfluency > Type mismatch: The type of disfluency is changed (e.g., repetition rendered as filler).

Only annotate errors that are clearly present.

SCORING

Provide two independent scores from 0 to 100.

1. Style Preservation Score (0–100)
Measures how well disfluencies are preserved (presence, type, placement, naturalness).

Guidance:
90–100: Nearly perfect preservation; natural and faithful.
70–89: Minor deviations.
40–69: Noticeable problems in preservation.
1–39: Major loss or distortion of disfluencies.

Meaning Preservation Score (0–100)
Measures accuracy and completeness of translated meaning.

Guidance:
90–100: Fully accurate and complete.
70–89: Minor inaccuracies.
40–69: Partial meaning loss.
1–39: Major meaning distortion or omission.

OUTPUT FORMAT (STRICT)

Return your evaluation in the following JSON format:

{
"errors": [
{
"category": "<Error Category>",
"description": "<Brief explanation>"
}
],
"style_preservation_score": <0-100>,
"meaning_preservation_score": <0-100>
}

If no errors are found, return:

{
"errors": [],
"style_preservation_score": <0-100>,
"meaning_preservation_score": <0-100>
}

Do not include any additional commentary outside the JSON.
